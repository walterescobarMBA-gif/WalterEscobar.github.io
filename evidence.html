Evidence 1: Uncoordinated Participant Relocations

Claim

Participant relocations occurred without proper coordination between sponsor and program staff.

Evidence

“These activities were not directly coordinated through APLA Health program staff at the time they occurred.”

Relevance

Confirms breakdown in oversight and coordination between sponsor and subcontractor during active housing decisions.

Impact: Demonstrates loss of program control during participant displacement events.

Evidence 2: Subcontractor Acting Without Sponsor Oversight

Claim

Subcontractor actions were not actively supervised by the program sponsor.

Evidence

APLA Health acknowledged that Housing & Supportive Services (HSS) initiated relocations independently.

Relevance

Supports finding that governance and internal controls were insufficient to ensure program compliance.

Impact: Participants were affected by decisions made outside structured program oversight.

Evidence 3: July 16 Letter Characterization vs. Participant Impact

Claim

Program communication included language perceived as coercive and tied to housing risk.

Evidence

“This notice was not disciplinary… you remain an active participant in good standing.”

Relevance

Shows discrepancy between sponsor’s interpretation and participant experience of pressure and risk.

Impact: Communication created uncertainty and perceived threat to housing stability.

Evidence 4: Flexible Program Limits Presented as Fixed

Claim

Program rules were applied as rigid limits despite later clarification that flexibility existed.

Evidence

LAHD guidance confirmed that the 24-month limit is a best practice, not a strict requirement.

Relevance

Supports finding that unwritten or misapplied rules affected participant outcomes.

Impact: Decisions impacting housing stability were based on incorrect or overstated constraints.

Evidence 5: Lack of Immediate Resolution Following Escalation

Claim

Escalation to program leadership did not result in timely corrective action.

Evidence

Sponsor response indicated ongoing internal review rather than immediate resolution.

Relevance

Demonstrates delay between issue identification and corrective response.

Impact: Housing instability persisted during review period without stabilization.

Conclusion

The evidence presented supports a pattern of administrative breakdown, inconsistent communication, and insufficient oversight during critical housing transitions. These documented materials align with audit findings and timeline events.
